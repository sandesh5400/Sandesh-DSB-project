### 🎯 Project Overview
This project is submitted as part of the **Data Science for Business (DSB)** module at the **University of Europe for Applied Sciences (UE), Potsdam**. The project investigates the historical behavior of three major global commodities — **Brent Crude Oil**, **Gold**, and **Silver** — alongside international currency payment trends tracked via the **SWIFT Currency Tracker**.

Global commodity markets are highly sensitive to geopolitical events, inflation cycles, monetary policy decisions, and macroeconomic shifts. Understanding these dynamics is critical for businesses, investors, and policymakers who rely on data-driven insights to manage risk and identify opportunities.

This project aims to:
- Analyze **long-term price trends** across commodities spanning over 100 years
- Identify **correlations and relationships** between Brent Oil, Gold, and Silver
- Measure **market volatility** during key historical periods
- Examine **global currency payment shares** and the growing role of emerging currencies such as the Chinese Yuan (RMB) in international transactions
- Derive **actionable business insights** from the data using statistical and visual analysis techniques

The analysis is conducted using Python-based data science tools including Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, and SciPy, with the full pipeline executed in a Jupyter Notebook environment on Kaggle.
